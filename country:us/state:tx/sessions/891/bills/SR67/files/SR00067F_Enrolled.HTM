SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 67
         WHEREAS, The Tyler Junior College men's tennis team
  achieved great distinction by winning the 2025 National Junior
  College Athletic Association Division I championship; and
         WHEREAS, Tyler joined the other top junior college teams
  from across the country at the NJCAA tournament, which took place
  from May 12 to 16 in Florence, South Carolina; the Apaches turned
  in a dominant performance, winning first place in singles flight
  No. 5 and sweeping the three doubles flights; TJC totaled 42
  points, 9 more than runner-up finisher Barton Community College,
  to capture the national crown for the second consecutive season
  and the 20th time in program history; and
         WHEREAS, Six Apache players garnered First Team
  All-American honors; Daniel Mauricio Marincas made the No. 5
  First Team Singles All-American squad and was a No. 3 First Team
  Doubles selection alongside Alex Morris; Daniel Borisov and Alex
  Despoja secured spots on the No. 1 Doubles squad, while Doni
  Gashi and Daniel Jankoski claimed All-American status on the
  No. 2 Doubles flight; TJC also received valuable contributions
  throughout the year from Jacopo Cianfarini, Ouga Miyazaki, and
  Alejandro Pardo; the squad was ably guided by interim head coach
  Micah Avance, who was named the NJCAA Coach of the Tournament;
  and
         WHEREAS, By once again earning the mantle of national
  champions, the Tyler Junior College Apaches have burnished the
  school's reputation as a tennis powerhouse, and all those
  associated with the team may indeed reflect with pride on a job
  well done; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby congratulate the Tyler
  Junior College men's tennis team on winning the 2025 NJCAA
  Division I championship and extend to the players, coaches, and
  staff sincere best wishes for continued success; and, be it
  further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for the team as an expression of high regard from the
  Texas Senate.
 
  Hughes
