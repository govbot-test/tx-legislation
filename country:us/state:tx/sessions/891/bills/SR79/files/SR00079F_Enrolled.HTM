SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 79
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Dewey McClain
 
         WHEREAS, A life made rich through meaningful service drew
  to a close with the passing of Dewey William McClain of Lone Star
  on July 24, 2025, at the age of 91; and
         WHEREAS, Dewey McClain was born in 1934, and he grew up
  with the companionship of four sisters, Loraine, Irene, Aletha,
  and Polly; answering his nation's call to duty, he enlisted in
  the United States Marine Corps; and
         WHEREAS, Following his military service, Mr. McClain
  embarked on a career at Lone Star Steel that spanned four
  decades; he also served as Justice of the Peace for Morris County
  for 24 years; and
         WHEREAS, In all his endeavors, Mr. McClain benefited from
  the love and support of his wife, Diane, with whom he shared 42
  years before her passing in 2012; he took great pride in their
  children, Kim, Micheal, and Brad, and he had the pleasure of
  seeing his treasured family grow to include 13 grandchildren and
  18 great-grandchildren; and
         WHEREAS, A man of faith, Mr. McClain was a lifelong member
  of the Daingerfield Church of Christ; in his leisure hours, he
  enjoyed hunting, camping, and fishing; he was at his happiest
  when he was spending time with his family in the outdoors; and
         WHEREAS, Although Dewey McClain will be deeply missed, he
  has left his loved ones with a wealth of memories that they will
  forever treasure; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the life
  of Dewey William "Bill" McClain and extend sincere condolences to
  the members of his family: to his children, Kim Hawkins, Micheal
  McClain and his wife, Tina, and Brad McClain and his wife,
  Brianna; to his grandchildren, Jonathan, Santos, Amanda, Alissa,
  Ryan, Andrew, Lainey, Tristan, Charley, Carley, Tatum, and
  Addison; to his great-grandchildren, Fiona, Emily, Baylor,
  Hadley, Penelope, Eleanor, Charlotte, Quentin, Maude, Beau,
  Hezekiah, Nezryn, Maisyn, Eli, Maddox, Peyton, Scotty, and
  Billy; and to his other relatives and friends; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Dewey McClain.
 
  Hughes
