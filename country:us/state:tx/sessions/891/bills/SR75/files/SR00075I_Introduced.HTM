R E S O L U T I O N
         WHEREAS, July 30, 2025, marks the 60th anniversary of the
  signing of the Medicare and Medicaid Act; and
         WHEREAS, On that day in 1965, President Lyndon B. Johnson
  signed the Medicare and Medicaid Act, also known as the Social
  Security Amendments of 1965, into law; the landmark bill
  established two programs: Medicare, which provides health
  insurance to citizens age 65 or older, and Medicaid, which provides
  health insurance to people with limited income; during the first
  three years following the passage of the legislation, nearly 20
  million beneficiaries were enrolled in the programs; and
         WHEREAS, Across the United States, 68.6 million individuals
  are currently enrolled in Medicare, and 71.1 million are enrolled
  in Medicaid, with approximately 12 million people enrolled in both
  programs; as of 2024, there were more than 4.7 million Texans
  receiving Medicare benefits, and in April 2025, over 4.1 million
  adults and children in Texas were enrolled in Medicaid or the
  Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP); and
         WHEREAS, Over the past six decades, the Medicare and Medicaid
  programs have made vital health care services more accessible to
  generations of Americans, and it is indeed a privilege to join in
  celebrating this important milestone; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the 89th Texas Legislature, 1st
  Called Session, hereby commemorate the 60th anniversary of the
  signing of the Medicare and Medicaid Act.
