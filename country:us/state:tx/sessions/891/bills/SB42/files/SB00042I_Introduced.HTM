A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
 
AN ACT
  relating to consent towing fees charged during a declared disaster.
         BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF TEXAS:
         SECTION 1.  Chapter 2308, Occupations Code, is amended by
  adding Subchapter L to read as follows:
  SUBCHAPTER L. MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS
         Sec. 2308.551.  CONSENT TOW FEES DURING DECLARED DISASTER.
  (a) A towing company may not take advantage of a disaster declared
  by the governor under Chapter 418, Government Code, or by the
  president of the United States by demanding an exorbitant or
  excessive price for a consent tow.
         (b)  A county judge may establish:
               (1)  the fees that a towing company may charge in
  connection with a consent tow during a declared disaster described
  by Subsection (a) in the county;
               (2)  the maximum amount a towing company may charge for
  fees, other than tow fees, in connection with a consent tow during a
  declared disaster described by Subsection (a) in the county; and
               (3)  a maximum amount that a towing company may charge
  for the following consent tows during a declared disaster described
  by Subsection (a) in the county:
                     (A)  standard light-duty tows of motor vehicles
  with a gross weight rating of 10,000 pounds or less;
                     (B)  medium-duty tows of motor vehicles with a
  gross weight rating of more than 10,000 pounds but less than 25,000
  pounds; and
                     (C)  heavy-duty tows of motor vehicles with a
  gross weight rating that exceeds 25,000 pounds.
         SECTION 2.  This Act takes effect on the 91st day after the
  last day of the legislative session.
