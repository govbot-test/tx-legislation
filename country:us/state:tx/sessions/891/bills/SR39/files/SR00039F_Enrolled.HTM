SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 39
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Claudette Jones
 
         WHEREAS, A full and generous life drew to a close with the
  passing of Claudette Cox Jones of Longview on July 4, 2025, at the
  age of 89; and
         WHEREAS, The daughter of Osa and Jack Cox, the former
  Claudette Cox was born in Kilgore on July 2, 1936, and she grew up
  with the companionship of four siblings; she graduated from
  Kilgore High School, and she went on earn a bachelor's degree in
  business administration from the University of North Texas and a
  master's degree from Stephen F. Austin State University; and
         WHEREAS, She wed James Clark Agee Jr. in 1957, and the
  couple became the parents of two daughters, Adana and Ami; after
  Mr. Agee's passing in 1970, she was fortunate enough to find love
  again and shared four rewarding decades of marriage with her
  husband, Cassity Jones, who also preceded her in death; the
  couple's blended family included Mr. Jones's four children, Jan,
  John, Jim, and Jerry, and through the years, she was further
  blessed with 14 grandchildren and 26 great-grandchildren; and
         WHEREAS, Actively engaged in her community, Mrs. Jones
  gave generously of her time to the Asbury House Child Enrichment
  Center for several decades; she also served as board president of
  the Texas Shakespeare Festival Foundation and was involved with
  the Good Shepherd Foundation and Goodwill Industries of the
  Pines; in recognition of her myriad volunteer service efforts,
  she was honored with numerous accolades and awards; and
         WHEREAS, A woman of strong faith, Mrs. Jones was a valued
  congregant of Longview First United Methodist Church, where she
  served on the board and participated in the Henry Foster Sunday
  school class; in her leisure hours, she enjoyed dancing,
  gardening, and hosting family gatherings, and she delighted in
  the time she spent traveling with her husband; moreover, she
  wrote and published a collection of poems entitled Grandmother's
  Garden at the age of 81; and
         WHEREAS, Although Claudette Jones is deeply missed, those
  who held her dear will forever remember the warmth and kindness
  she brought into their lives; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory
  of Claudette Cox Jones and extend sincere condolences to the
  members of her family: to her children, Adana Peloubet and her
  husband, John, Ami Cooper and her husband, Craig, Jan Forester
  and her husband, Sam, John Jones and his wife, Lisa, Jim Jones and
  his wife, Vicki, and Jerry Jones and his wife, Lisa; to her
  grandchildren and great-grandchildren; and to her other
  relatives and friends; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for her family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Claudette Jones.
 
  Hughes
