89(1) SR 33 - Enrolled version - Bill Text SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 33 In Memory of William Cole Jr. WHEREAS, Family and friends will forever cherish their memories of William Eugene Cole Jr. of Atlanta, who passed away on May 11, 2025, at the age of 96; and WHEREAS, The son of William Eugene Cole and Fannie Mae Cole Phillips, Bill Cole was born on October 15, 1928, in Miami, Florida, and he grew up with the companionship of five siblings, Norman, Margie, Leon, Troy, and Shirley; and WHEREAS, On December 16, 1950, Mr. Cole married the former Alverta Knocke, and the couple was blessed with three children, Judy, Randy, and Larry; after that marriage ended, he was fortunate enough to find love again with his second wife, Joyce; with the passing years, he saw his treasured family grow to include five grandchildren and three great-grandchildren; and WHEREAS, Over the course of his career in the oil and gas industry, Mr. Cole worked for Shell Oil Company, Aramco, and Halliburton; he also was a cattle rancher for many years, and he coached Little League baseball; in his leisure time, he took pleasure in attending Atlanta Rabbits football games and playing cards and dominoes; a man of faith, he was a member of First Baptist Church Atlanta; and WHEREAS, Bill Cole enjoyed a life that was rich in the intangibles that matter most, and he is deeply missed by all those who held him dear; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of William Eugene Cole Jr. and extend sincere condolences to the members of his family: to his wife, Joyce; to his children, Judy Cole Cook and her husband, John, and Randy Cole; to his grandchildren, Stephanie Cole Rink, Lance Cole, Cody Cole, Katie Kotzur, and Stephanie Cook Perry; to his great-grandchildren, Harper Rink, Jaywan Cole, and Jonah Kotzur; to his siblings, Leon, Troy, and Shirley Phillips; and to his other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Bill Cole. Hughes ________________________________ President of the Senate I hereby certify that the above Resolution was adopted by the Senate on August 15, 2025, by a rising vote. ________________________________ Secretary of the Senate ________________________________ Member, Texas Senate
