SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 69
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Billy Pyron
 
         WHEREAS, Family and friends will long cherish their
  memories of Billy Daryl Pyron of Tyler, who passed away on May 18,
  2025, at the age of 85; and
         WHEREAS, Born in Tyler on November 20, 1939, to Eddie and
  Ona Pyron, Billy Pyron grew up with the companionship of his
  brothers, Bobby and Mike; he was a member of the Tyler High School
  Class of 1958 and went on to attend Tyler Junior College; and
         WHEREAS, Mr. Pyron enjoyed a rewarding career in
  commercial real estate; his many efforts included founding Pyron
  Investments, and he was instrumental in the development of south
  Tyler; active in the community, he was a member of the Cedars of
  Lebanon and the Willow Brook and Hollytree Country Clubs as well
  as a proud supporter of St. Jude Children's Research Hospital and
  the Tunnel to Towers Foundation; a man of faith, he grew up as a
  member of Central Baptist Church, and he later attended Cathedral
  of the Immaculate Conception for many years; and
         WHEREAS, Mr. Pyron was the proud father of four children,
  Merilyn, Shelley, Rhonda, and Billy, and with the passing years,
  he delighted in becoming "Big Daddy" to his six grandchildren,
  Hunter, Morgan, Clayton, Carley, Cole, and Billy, and to his
  great-grandchildren; in all his endeavors, he benefited from the
  love and support of his longtime companion, Judith; he treasured
  spending time with his loved ones, especially at Lake Palestine
  and on family trips in the motorhome; he also enjoyed collecting
  unique sports cars and rooting for the Dallas Cowboys and the
  Texas Longhorns; and
         WHEREAS, Admired for his generosity, his wonderful sense
  of humor, and his devotion to his family and friends, Billy Pyron
  lived a life that was rich in personal and professional
  achievements, and he will forever be a source of inspiration to
  those he leaves behind; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory
  of Billy Daryl Pyron and extend sincere condolences to the
  members of his family: to his longtime companion, Judith D.
  Elliott; to his daughters, Merilyn Hartley, Shelley Simpson and
  her husband, John, and Rhonda Overbergen and her husband, Todd;
  to his children's mother, Merigale Manziel Pyron; to his
  grandchildren, Hunter Hartley and his wife, Jennifer, Morgan
  Hartley and her husband, William, Clayton, Carley, and Cole
  Overbergen, and Billy Pyron III; to his great-grandchildren; and
  to his many other relatives and friends; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Billy Pyron.
 
  Hughes
