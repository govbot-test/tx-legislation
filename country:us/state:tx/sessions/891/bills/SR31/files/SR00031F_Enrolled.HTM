SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 31
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  George Caldwell
 
         WHEREAS, Family and friends were deeply saddened by the
  passing of George Edward Caldwell of Tyler on April 4, 2025, at
  the age of 88; and
         WHEREAS, The son of George and Evelyn Caldwell, George
  Caldwell was born on September 24, 1936, and he grew up with the
  companionship of a brother, Robert, and two sisters, Jeanette and
  Frances; he graduated from Tyler High School and went on to
  attend Tyler Junior College; answering his nation's call to duty,
  he served honorably in the United States Army; and
         WHEREAS, Mr. Caldwell enjoyed a rewarding career in the
  newspaper and hospital industries, and he retired in 2003; his
  many interests included motorcycles, baseball, and football;
  above all else, he cherished time spent with his family and his
  Boston terrier, Buddy, and he looked forward to his monthly
  lunches with his former high school classmates; he was also a man
  of strong faith; and
         WHEREAS, In all his endeavors, Mr. Caldwell benefited from
  the love and support of his wife, Linda, his son, Eddie, and his
  daughters, Kathy and Pamela; through the years, his family grew
  to include five grandchildren, eight great-grandchildren, and a
  great-great-grandson; and
         WHEREAS, George Caldwell enjoyed a life that was rich in
  the intangibles that matter most, and he is deeply missed by all
  those who held him dear; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory
  of George Edward Caldwell and extend deepest condolences to the
  members of his family: to his wife, Linda Caldwell; to his son,
  George Edward Caldwell Jr., and his wife, Lisa; to his daughters,
  Kathy Caldwell Ramsey and Pamela Pegram, and her husband, James;
  to his grandchildren, Melissa Anderson, Taylor Hopper, Tera
  Lorance, Tyler Caldwell, and Taylor Caldwell; to his
  great-grandchildren and great-great-grandson; and to his other
  relatives and friends; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of George Caldwell.
 
  Hughes
