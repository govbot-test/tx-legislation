SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 29
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Amanda Wallace
 
         WHEREAS, The family and friends of Amanda Dawn Wallace
  suffered an immeasurable loss with her death on February 6, 2025,
  at the age of 46; and
         WHEREAS, The daughter of Edward and Beatrice Wallace,
  Amanda Wallace was born in Tyler on February 23, 1978, and she
  grew up with the companionship of a brother, Jeffrey; she lived
  in Mineola throughout her early youth, where she further
  benefited from the kinship of a large extended family that
  included a number of maternal and paternal cousins, aunts, and
  uncles; as a teenager, she moved with her family to Amarillo, and
  she later graduated from Scott Community College in Bettendorf,
  Iowa, with a degree in dental hygiene; and
         WHEREAS, Ms. Wallace shared a rewarding 25-year
  partnership with her fiancé, Jason Epton; she took great pride in
  her children, Elijah and Gabriella, and with the passing years
  she had the pleasure of seeing her treasured family grow to
  include a grandson, Gianni; and
         WHEREAS, A kind and compassionate woman who never met a
  stranger, Ms. Wallace found great fulfillment in her many
  friendships; in her leisure hours, she enjoyed spending time
  outdoors and cheering on the Green Bay Packers with her fiancé;
  above all else, she cherished the time she spent with her family,
  to whom she provided immense love and support; and
         WHEREAS, Those fortunate enough to have known Amanda
  Wallace will remember the way she touched theirliveswith her
  generosity, optimism, and humor, and she will forever hold a
  treasured place in their hearts; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory
  of Amanda Dawn Wallace and extend sincere condolences to the
  members of her family: to her children, Elijah Wallace and his
  wife, Cadence, and Gabriella Delcenia Cruz Trevino; to her
  grandson, Gianni Cruz Placensia; to her fiancé, Jason Epton; to
  her brother, Jeffrey Wallace; and to her other relatives and
  friends; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for her family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Amanda Wallace.
 
  Hughes
