SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 38
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Dorothy West
 
         WHEREAS, Dorothy Woodruff West of Longview, an esteemed
  civic leader and a friend to many, passed away on April 30, 2025,
  at the age of 85; and
         WHEREAS, The former "Dot" Graves was born to Floyd and
  Audrey Graves on September 12, 1939, in Atlanta; a 1958 graduate
  of Marshall High School, she attended business school and was
  employed by Southwestern Electric Power Company, where she met
  her husband, Donald Ray Woodruff; they married in 1960 and lived
  in Marshall, where they raised two children, Mike and Laura, and
  shared 35 happy years before Mr. Woodruff's death; she was
  fortunate enough to find love again and wed Jeff West Sr. in 1998;
  residents of Longview, the couple spent a decade together until
  Mr. West's passing; and
         WHEREAS, Mrs. West served as a secretary at Wesley United
  Methodist Church in Marshall until 1980, when she was named
  administrative secretary to the dean of student affairs at East
  Texas Baptist University; she earned a bachelor's degree while
  working full-time, and she subsequently became director of the
  campus student center, bookstore, and post office; after
  obtaining an MBA in 1995, she served as an adjunct professor of
  marketing; she retired from ETBU in 2004; and
         WHEREAS, Over the decades, Mrs. West made invaluable
  contributions to her fellow citizens; she worked with the
  Marshall Council of Camp Fire, and she received the Luther Halsey
  Gulick Award for extraordinary council leadership; continuing to
  work with youth, she formed the Rainbow Blue Birds Group for
  girls, and she was presented with honorary life membership in the
  Texas Congress of Parents and Teachers by the William B. Travis
  Elementary PTA of Marshall; and
         WHEREAS, Deeply dedicated to her family, her faith, and her
  community, Dot West earned the lasting respect and admiration of
  all who were privileged to know her; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the life
  of Dorothy Woodruff West and extend heartfelt condolences to the
  members of her family:  to her children, Mike Woodruff and his
  wife, Thelma, and Laura Woodruff; to her grandson, Josh West; to
  her nephews, Steve Woodruff, Gil George, and Chris George, and
  her niece, Sheri Eudaly; and to her many other relatives and
  friends; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for her family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Dot West.
 
  Hughes
