SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 42
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Dale Hall
 
         WHEREAS, A life filled with joyful times and meaningful
  accomplishments drew to a close with the passing of Dale Eston
  Hall on May 5, 2025, at the age of 61; and
         WHEREAS, The son of Mary and Harlan Hall, Dale Hall was
  born in Odessa on August 15, 1963, and he grew up in Longview with
  the companionship of his sister, Diane; he attended Longview High
  School, where his musical talents were demonstrated as a trombone
  player in the school band; following graduation, he went on to
  attend Austin Community College; and
         WHEREAS, Mr. Hall enjoyed a long and fulfilling career as a
  computer specialist for the State of Texas, and his efforts were
  recognized with multiple employee of the month awards; and
         WHEREAS, A man of strong faith, Mr. Hall was a valued
  member of First Baptist Church Longview and Hyde Park Church in
  Austin; known for his keen wit, altruistic nature, and infectious
  smile, he delighted in helping others, and he had a great love for
  animals, including his cherished dog, Patches; in his leisure
  hours, he enjoyed cooking and grilling; and
         WHEREAS, Dale Hall possessed a generosity of spirit that
  expressed itself in myriad ways, and he enriched the lives of
  countless people with his warmth and kindness; now, therefore, be
  it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory
  of Dale Eston Hall and extend sincere condolences to the members
  of his family: to his father, Harlan Hall; to his sister, Diane
  Rice and her husband, Mark; to his nephew, Justin Rice; and to his
  other relatives and many friends; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Dale Hall.
 
  Hughes
