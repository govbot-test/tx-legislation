89(1) SR 88 - Enrolled version - Bill Text SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 88 In Memory of Willie Brown WHEREAS, A life made rich through meaningful service drew to a close with the passing of Willie Charles Brown of Troup on June 19, 2025, at the age of 84; and WHEREAS, The son of Willie Winston Brown and Georgia Lee Broughton, "Joe Boy" Brown was born in Whitehouse on September 17, 1940; after graduating from Arp Industrial High School, he completed his bachelor's degree at Prairie View A&M University, where he balanced academic studies with many hours of hands-on work at the institution's farm; he went on to earn a master's degree at Texas A&M University; and WHEREAS, Mr. Brown proudly served his country in the U.S. Army for nearly three decades, and he cherished the lasting bonds he formed with his comrades; over the years, he often shared stories, whether humorous or profound, from his days as a soldier; his military discipline and leadership carried over to his civilian career as a construction foreman; the projects he worked on included several of the most notable buildings in Houston, and he was dedicated to ensuring fine craftsmanship and attention to detail; following his retirement from the army in 1994, he gave back to his community as an agriculture teacher; he had a great love for the land and enjoyed tending his garden, managing his herd of cattle, and spending peaceful hours on his farm; and WHEREAS, For more than half a century, Mr. Brown served as a deacon, most recently at New Canaan Baptist Church in Whitehouse; he inspired others through his wisdom, compassion, and unwavering commitment to his faith and his family; in all his endeavors, he was supported by the love and encouragement of his wife, Beverly J. Brown, who preceded him in death, and he was a devoted father to his seven children, Raquel, Jennifer, Ashley, Kimberley, Marcus, Anthony, and Byron; and WHEREAS, Although Joe Boy Brown is deeply missed, those who held him dear will forever remember the warmth and kindness that he brought into their lives; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Willie Charles "Joe Boy" Brown and extend sincere sympathy to the members of his family: to his siblings, Johnnie Johnson, James Brown and his wife, Velma, LeBertha Mitchell and her husband, Rayford, Ella Brown, Marilyn Griffith and her husband, Kirby, and David Brown and his wife, Maurita; to his children, Raquel Harrison and her husband, Sha-Michael, Jennifer Brown, Ashley Brown and her husband, Johnathan, Kimberly Brown, Marcus Brown and his wife, Julia, Anthony Brown, and Byron Brown; to his grandchildren, Brandy Walker, David, Brianna, and Brittany Carter, Jace and Serena Brown, and Ashton Lincoln; to his great-grandchildren, Mason and Kori Smith; and to his other relatives and friends; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Joe Boy Brown. Hughes ________________________________ President of the Senate I hereby certify that the above Resolution was adopted by the Senate on August 15, 2025, by a rising vote. ________________________________ Secretary of the Senate ________________________________ Member, Texas Senate
