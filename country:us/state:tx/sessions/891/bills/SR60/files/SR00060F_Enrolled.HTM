SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 60
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Bobby Roberts Sr.
 
         WHEREAS, A full and generous life drew to a close with the
  passing of Dr. Bobby Gene Roberts Sr. of Tyler on June 13, 2025,
  at the age of 90; and
         WHEREAS, The son of Clyde Floyd Roberts and Johnnie Mae
  Davis, Bob Roberts was born in San Augustine on May 26, 1935, and
  he grew up with the companionship of two sisters, Linda and
  Billie; first called to the ministry as a teenager, he was
  ordained as a Southern Baptist minister in 1955; and
         WHEREAS, A graduate of Baylor University, Pastor Roberts
  also attended Lamar University and Southwestern Baptist
  Theological Seminary, and he earned master and doctor of divinity
  degrees from Luther Rice Seminary; his first full-time pastorate
  was to Woodrow Baptist Church in Silsbee, and he went on to serve
  churches in Winnie, Corsicana, Wichita Falls, Port Acres, and
  Lindale; and
         WHEREAS, After leaving the pastorate in 1979 to devote
  himself to evangelism and mission work, Dr. Roberts founded
  Christian Foundations of Faith, Inc.; Belize became his primary
  mission field, where he built churches, sponsored ministries,
  and supported local preachers through resources and mentorship;
  over the years, he became well-known for his "Sixty Second
  Sermon" devotionals, newsletters, and television broadcasts that
  reached countless people in North and Central America; and
         WHEREAS, In all his endeavors, Pastor Roberts enjoyed the
  love and support of his wife of 71 years, Gay O'Keefe Roberts; he
  was the proud father of their four children, Sandra, Bob, Mark,
  and Sheila, and with the passing years, their family grew to
  include six grandchildren, Bethany, Samuel, Benjamin, Jill,
  Grace, and Clara, and six great-grandchildren, Moses, Esther,
  Luke, Naomi, Zelda, and Ronen; and
         WHEREAS, While the passing of Bob Roberts brings great
  sadness to his family and friends, he leaves behind a legacy that
  will long be treasured by all those who held him dear; now,
  therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory
  of Dr. Bobby Gene Roberts Sr. and extend sincere condolences to
  all who mourn his passing; and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Dr. Bob Roberts.
 
  Hughes
