89(1) SR 60 - Introduced version - Bill Text 89S10550 KRT-D By: Hughes S.R. No. 60 R E S O L U T I O N WHEREAS, A full and generous life drew to a close with the passing of Dr. Bobby Gene Roberts Sr. of Tyler on June 13, 2025, at the age of 90; and WHEREAS, The son of Clyde Floyd Roberts and Johnnie Mae Davis, Bob Roberts was born in San Augustine on May 26, 1935, and he grew up with the companionship of two sisters, Linda and Billie; first called to the ministry as a teenager, he was ordained as a Southern Baptist minister in 1955; and WHEREAS, A graduate of Baylor University, Pastor Roberts also attended Lamar University and Southwestern Baptist Theological Seminary, and he earned master and doctor of divinity degrees from Luther Rice Seminary; his first full-time pastorate was to Woodrow Baptist Church in Silsbee, and he went on to serve churches in Winnie, Corsicana, Wichita Falls, Port Acres, and Lindale; and WHEREAS, After leaving the pastorate in 1979 to devote himself to evangelism and mission work, Dr. Roberts founded Christian Foundations of Faith, Inc.; Belize became his primary mission field, where he built churches, sponsored ministries, and supported local preachers through resources and mentorship; over the years, he became well-known for his "Sixty Second Sermon" devotionals, newsletters, and television broadcasts that reached countless people in North and Central America; and WHEREAS, In all his endeavors, Pastor Roberts enjoyed the love and support of his wife of 71 years, Gay O'Keefe Roberts; he was the proud father of their four children, Sandra, Bob, Mark, and Sheila, and with the passing years, their family grew to include six grandchildren, Bethany, Samuel, Benjamin, Jill, Grace, and Clara, and six great-grandchildren, Moses, Esther, Luke, Naomi, Zelda, and Ronen; and WHEREAS, While the passing of Bob Roberts brings great sadness to his family and friends, he leaves behind a legacy that will long be treasured by all those who held him dear; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That the Senate of the 89th Texas Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory of Dr. Bobby Gene Roberts Sr. and extend sincere condolences to all who mourn his passing; and, be it further RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns this day, it do so in memory of Dr. Bob Roberts.
