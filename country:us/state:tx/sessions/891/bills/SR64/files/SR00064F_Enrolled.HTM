SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 64
 
 
  In Memory
  of
  Charles Narramore
 
         WHEREAS, Memories of a life well lived may help to comfort
  those saddened by the death of Charles Augustus Narramore of
  Longview on July 20, 2025, at the age of 97; and
         WHEREAS, Charles Narramore was born in Georges Creek,
  Arkansas, on March 7, 1928, to Charles Roscoe Narramore and Essie
  Pierce Narramore; he grew up with the companionship of two
  brothers, Frank and Jack, and a sister, Sybil; and
         WHEREAS, Mr. Narramore enjoyed a rewarding career as a
  steelworker; he was a longtime resident of Diana and was active
  in his community as a member of Masonic Lodges Ashland No. 1138
  and Little Cypress No. 1459; he was also a man of faith and a
  valued congregant of Smyrna Methodist Church; his many interests
  included writing, singing, researching genealogy, hunting, and
  performing mechanical work; and
         WHEREAS, Although he will be deeply missed, Charles
  Narramore leaves behind numerous friends and loved ones whose
  lives are far richer for having known him, and he will always hold
  a treasured place in their hearts; now, therefore, be it
         RESOLVED, That the Senate of the State of Texas, 89th
  Legislature, 1st Called Session, hereby pay tribute to the memory
  of Charles Augustus Narramore and extend heartfelt condolences
  to the members of his family: to his children, Jimmy Narramore
  and his wife, Debbie, Russell Narramore, Anita N. Hurst and her
  husband, Sebastian, Bobby Dehart and his wife, Patsy, Reba Dugger
  and her husband, Steve, Barbara McCay, Sharon Pilcher, and
  Lynette Peterson; to his 25 grandchildren and many
  great-grandchildren; and to his other relatives and friends;
  and, be it further
         RESOLVED, That an official copy of this Resolution be
  prepared for his family and that when the Texas Senate adjourns
  this day, it do so in memory of Charles Narramore.
 
  Hughes
